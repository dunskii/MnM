## Getting Started

### Logging In

1. Navigate to `https://app.musicnme.com.au`
2. Enter your email address (provided by your admin)
3. Enter your password
4. Click **Login**

### First Login

On your first login, you may be prompted to:
- Change your temporary password
- Update your profile information
- Review your assigned instruments

### Your Permissions

As a teacher, you can:
- ✅ View **ALL** lessons and students in the school
- ✅ Mark attendance for **any** lesson
- ✅ Add notes for students and classes
- ✅ Upload resources to lessons
- ✅ View Google Drive linked folders
- ❌ Create or delete lessons (admin only)
- ❌ Manage invoices or payments (admin only)
- ❌ Approve Meet & Greets (admin only)

## Viewing Your Schedule

### Calendar View

1. Click **Calendar** in the navigation
2. You'll see all lessons (your lessons are highlighted)
3. Use filters to narrow down:
   - **My Lessons Only** - Toggle to show only your lessons
   - **Term** - Select term
   - **Location** - Filter by location

### Calendar Colors

| Color | Lesson Type |
|-------|-------------|
| Blue (#4580E4) | Individual lessons |
| Yellow (#FFCE00) | Group lessons |
| Mint (#96DAC9) | Band lessons |
| Coral (#FFAE9E) | Hybrid lessons |

### Viewing Lesson Details

Click on any lesson to see:
- Lesson name and type
- Time and duration
- Room
- Teacher
- Enrolled students
- Resources attached

### Week View vs Month View

- **Week View** - Best for daily planning
- **Month View** - Best for overview

Toggle between views using the buttons at the top of the calendar.

## Marking Attendance

### Why Attendance Matters

- Tracks student participation
- Helps parents see progress
- Required for billing accuracy
- Identifies students needing support

### Marking Attendance for a Session

1. Go to **Attendance** or click on a lesson
2. Select the date of the session
3. For each student, click:
   - ✅ **Present** - Student attended
   - ❌ **Absent** - Student did not attend
   - ⏰ **Late** - Student arrived late
4. Click **Save Attendance**

### Quick Mark All

For group lessons:
1. Click **Mark All Present**
2. Then individually mark any absences or late arrivals

### Viewing Attendance History

1. Open a lesson
2. Click **Attendance History**
3. View past sessions and attendance records

### Attendance Statistics

Each lesson shows:
- Overall attendance rate
- Individual student attendance rates
- Absent patterns

## Adding Notes

### Note Types

**Class Notes** - General notes about a lesson session
- What was covered
- Group dynamics
- Upcoming plans

**Student Notes** - Individual notes for specific students
- Progress observations
- Areas for improvement
- Achievements
- Parent communication needed

### Adding Class Notes

1. Open a lesson
2. Click **Notes** tab
3. Click **+ Add Class Note**
4. Select the session date
5. Write your note
6. Click **Save**

### Adding Student Notes

1. Open a lesson
2. Click **Notes** tab
3. Click **+ Add Student Note**
4. Select the student
5. Select the session date
6. Write your note
7. Click **Save**

### Notes Requirement

**Important:** Notes are expected:
- Daily ideally
- By end of week at latest

Your dashboard shows "Notes Due" to help you track this.

### Best Practices for Notes

**Good note example:**
> "Emily showed great improvement in her finger positioning today. She successfully played the C major scale with correct hand shape. Recommend focusing on tempo consistency next week. Ready to start simple melodies."

**Avoid:**
> "Good lesson" (too vague)
> "Needs work" (not specific enough)

## Managing Resources

### Viewing Resources

1. Open a lesson
2. Click **Resources** tab
3. View all files attached to the lesson

### Uploading Resources

1. Open a lesson
2. Click **Resources** tab
3. Click **+ Upload File**
4. Select file(s) from your computer
5. Set visibility:
   - **All** - Students, parents, and teachers
   - **Teachers & Parents** - Not visible to students
   - **Teachers Only** - Only visible to teachers
6. Click **Upload**

### Supported File Types

- **Documents:** PDF, Word, Excel
- **Images:** JPG, PNG, GIF
- **Audio:** MP3, WAV
- **Video:** MP4 (small files only)

**Maximum file size:** 25 MB

### Google Drive Integration

If your school has connected Google Drive:

1. Files uploaded sync to Drive automatically
2. Files added to Drive appear in the portal
3. Sync happens every 15 minutes

### Organizing Resources

Tips for keeping resources organized:
- Use clear, descriptive file names
- Include student name for individual resources
- Include date for session-specific materials

## Hybrid Lessons

### Understanding Hybrid Lessons

Hybrid lessons alternate between:
- **Group weeks** - All students together (60 min)
- **Individual weeks** - One-on-one sessions (45 min)

### Your Role in Hybrid Lessons

During **Group Weeks:**
- Teach all enrolled students together
- Mark attendance as usual
- Add class notes

During **Individual Weeks:**
- Students have booked specific time slots
- View your schedule for their bookings
- Mark attendance for each individual session
- Add student-specific notes

### Viewing Hybrid Schedules

1. Open a hybrid lesson
2. View the **Pattern** section:
   - Shows which weeks are group vs individual
3. Click **View Bookings** to see individual session bookings

### Calendar Display

- **Group weeks** - Show as single event with all students
- **Individual weeks** - Show as separate events per student

## Viewing Students

### Accessing Student Information

As a teacher, you can view all students in the school. This helps when:
- Covering for another teacher
- Understanding a student's history
- Coordinating across lessons

### Student Profile

Each student profile shows:
- **Personal Info** - Name, age, instrument
- **Lessons** - All enrolled lessons
- **Attendance** - Attendance history
- **Notes** - Notes from all teachers
- **Resources** - Files shared with student

### Viewing Your Students

1. Go to **Students**
2. Use filter **My Students** to see only students in your lessons
3. Click on any student for details

### Student Age Groups

Students are categorized by age:
- **Pre-school** (Alice character) - Under 5
- **Kids** (Steve character) - 5-12 years
- **Teens** (Liam character) - 13-17 years
- **Adults** (Floyd character) - 18+ years

These characters appear on student profiles for quick identification.

## Tips & Best Practices

### Daily Routine

1. **Start of day:** Check your dashboard for today's lessons
2. **Before each lesson:** Review student notes and last session
3. **After each lesson:** Mark attendance immediately
4. **End of day:** Add any pending notes

### Weekly Routine

1. **Monday:** Review the week's schedule
2. **Friday:** Ensure all notes are complete
3. **Weekend:** Prepare resources for next week

### Communication

- Add notes that would be helpful for parents
- Flag any concerns for admin
- Coordinate with other teachers teaching the same student

### Covering for Other Teachers

Since you can view all lessons:
1. Check the lesson's recent notes
2. Review student attendance patterns
3. Continue from where the regular teacher left off
4. Add notes about what you covered

### Resource Sharing

- Share useful resources across similar lessons
- Use consistent file naming
- Set appropriate visibility (parents may not need teacher prep materials)

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**"Can't find my lessons"**
- Check the date range on the calendar
- Ensure the correct term is selected
- Try clearing filters

**"Attendance won't save"**
- Ensure you've selected a status for each student
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page

**"Can't upload file"**
- Check file size (max 25 MB)
- Verify file type is supported
- Try a different browser

**"Notes not showing"**
- Ensure you selected the correct date
- Check if viewing class notes vs student notes
- Refresh the page

**"Can't see student details"**
- Student may have been deleted
- Check if viewing correct school
- Contact admin if issue persists

### Getting Help

- **Admin Issues:** Contact your school administrator
- **Technical Issues:** support@musicnme.com.au
- **In-App Help:** Click the ? icon
